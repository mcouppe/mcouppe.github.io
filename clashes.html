TECHNICAL DESCRIPTION

camera: Canon EOS 500n
lens : Canon 35-80mm
film :Ilford HP5 400 ISO, Kodak Gold 200 ISO
place : Paris, France
date : 2016-2017

At the dawn of spring 2016, many protests are breaking out all over France and especially in Paris. The issue ? The "Loi Travail", a law ordered under the government of François Hollande and carried by Emmanuel Macron, a law leaving a lot of workers more precarious and deleting several workers protections . Thus, as soon as March 2016, wild demonstrations led by students and high-schoolers burst to the Eiffel Tower while the Place de la République will become the theatre of many gatherings under the label of "Nuit Debout" .

The first processions marching through the streets are similar to so many others seen so far in the landscape of French militancy. But as time went by, tensions with the police forces and the government are growing and a still rare phenomenon start appearing in protests : the " Cortege de Tête ". The protesters are no longer here to march passively while the union trucks play pop music as it's usual in France. It's therefore a matter of struggle.
Windows fall down, masks cover faces, smoke rises into the sky and city squares are reclaimed by demonstrators as the sun sets.

As the months go by, there are at least two protests a week and the pace is accelerating. The government and the police forces then tighten the noose until the famous "49.3", the passage in force of the law in the National Assembly. The people inside the "Cortège de tête" start covering themselves in black and the first massive Black Blocks appear in France . This technique from Germany helps the demonstrators to express their anger against capitalism and state power by targeting banks and multinationals alongside the protests, while at the same time repelling police officers who try to stop the processions even before they left, notably with mass arrest missions and an increase in police violent actions .
