TECHNICAL DESCRIPTION

camera : Nikon D3200, Canon 5Dmk III and Canon EOS 500n
lens : Nikon 18-105mm, Canon 24-105mm and Canon 35-80mm

In this section, you will find various shots taken over the years and during my adventures. Whether they are scenes of everyday life or "souvenir photography" , it is important for me to constantly try to freeze/store colours, lights and atmospheres. Thus, from the calm water on the Hastings waterfront to this bed edge slightly eroded by time, I am always looking for the perfect reproduction of the effect of light on the texture . The building under demolition is slightly yellowed by a setting sun filtered by clouds but also by the layer of pollution and dust that covers it. It is this combination of added elements that creates this aesthetic effect. And it is thanks to the accumulation of these physical effects that we arrive at the photograph of the station platform where we can perceive the waves of steam due to the heat wave on concrete and iron. In this way, photography is no longer just a vehicle for scenes and events, but becomes a carrier of an atmosphere that agitates our sensory capacities.

These two photographs of overflowing coloured liquid are the result of a partnership with Broncolor. Here, a physical phenomenon is frozen by the strobe of light. The drops that explode, the liquid that overflows from its container are all micro-events that appeal to the senses and our ability to perceive matter and its gravity.

Finally, I also used light and colour as tools to express emotions. The light underlines the features while the colour brings the liveliness of the expression: is it a catch on the spot with rosier lips and more colourful cheeks or is it a more diffuse feeling, causing skin discolouration?
